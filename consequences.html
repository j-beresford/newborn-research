Consequences

A section on 'time out' and 'points charts' is coming, but ideally we never have to use these. Instead we just ignore bad behaviours - maybe by leaving the room - and praise good behaviours. All praise is good and to be encouraged, but it's not all created equal either, there are three components of 'special praise':

  1. Verbal praise: fantastic!
  2. State the exact behaviour: you put on your shoes as soon as I asked!
  3. Physical contact: pat, hug, kiss etc.

A few things to avoid:

  1. NegComs: "That was great, why cant you always be that good?!"
  2. Conditional affection: "Mummy and Daddy really love you when you tidy your room" - no, we ALWAYS love you.
  3. Vagueness and delays: "you were so good last weekend". Not harmful, but not really helpful either.
